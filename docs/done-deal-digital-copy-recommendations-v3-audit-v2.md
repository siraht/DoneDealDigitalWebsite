## What Changed In v2

This pass is tighter, safer, and more decisive than the first audit.

- More cuts, fewer slogans
- More owner-language, less agency-language
- More emphasis on proof restraint and qualified claims
- Earlier trust and clearer next steps
- Cleaner distinction between what should be public now and what still depends on owner decisions

## Shared Standards

Every recommendation below is scored against these questions:

- Does the page immediately say who it is for?
- Does each section tie back to a business outcome?
- Is trust visible early enough?
- Is the CTA right for the page?
- Are claims supportable or properly qualified?
- Are included vs extra items defined where needed?
- Can a busy owner understand it in 30 seconds?
- Does it sound like a competent operator?

## Priority Order

1. Rewrite homepage first.
2. Rewrite Local SEO second.
3. Remove or unpublish Case Studies immediately.
4. Replace legal pages with factual route-specific versions before launch.
5. Tighten Lead Generation next.
6. Then refine Web Design, About, FAQ, Contact, Thank-You, and 404.

## Remaining Unresolved Business Decisions

- Whether website pricing stays custom-only or moves to a public `starts at` number
- Whether hosting/maintenance is included, optional, or separate
- Geographic scope language once the public market focus is final
- Whether ongoing SEO and paid-ads minimums will be public
- Exact client ownership wording for domains, analytics, ad accounts, and site files
- When proof thresholds are met for a public Case Studies route
- Which third-party processors and legal contact details are actually live at launch

## Claims Removed Or Softened In v2

- `dominate local search`
- `first page of Google`
- `market leader`
- `booked solid`
- hard implied ranking promises
- hard implied lead-volume promises
- unconfirmed contract or response-time promises
- unconfirmed hosting/pricing ownership claims
- directory-quantity framing as a proxy for SEO value
- public proof language that relies on fake or unapproved case-study signals

## Best Overall Direction

- Homepage should make Web Design the clearest first step.
- Every page should read like it was written for an owner between jobs.
- Trust should come from clarity, scope, process, and honest limits.
- When the business has not decided something yet, the copy should stay honest and light instead of filling the gap with invented certainty.
